# Deep Research Methodology

## Purpose
Systematic approach to multi-source research that ensures thoroughness and accuracy.

## Methodology

### Step 1: Scope the Research
- Identify the core question(s) to answer
- Determine the depth needed (quick lookup vs deep dive)
- List key terms and synonyms for searching

### Step 2: Gather Sources
- Use multiple search engines and approaches
- Search with different phrasings of the same question
- Include both recent and authoritative sources
- Look for primary sources (original papers, official docs) over secondary (blog posts)

### Step 3: Cross-Reference
- Verify claims across at least 2-3 independent sources
- Note any contradictions or disagreements
- Check publication dates for currency
- Identify potential biases in sources

### Step 4: Synthesize Findings
- Organize findings by subtopic
- Clearly distinguish facts from speculation
- Note confidence levels (high/medium/low)
- Identify gaps in available information

### Step 5: Present Results
- Lead with the answer to the core question
- Provide supporting evidence with citations
- Note any caveats or limitations
- Suggest follow-up research if needed

## Quick Reference
- Always cite sources with URLs
- Prefer primary sources over secondary
- Cross-reference claims across 2+ sources
- Note contradictions explicitly
- Distinguish facts from speculation
